CO₂ Transport Resilience

ETH Zürich - Zurich, Switzerland

Fall 2025

Developed a techno-economic framework to evaluate resilience strategies in CO₂ transport networks under infrastructure failure scenarios. The model integrates transport modes (pipeline, truck, rail, ship), temporary storage, and direct emissions, accounting for dynamic carbon pricing, failure duration, and system utilization. Results show that resilience strategies are not universally optimal, but become economically viable under prolonged disruptions and high utilization. Temporary CO₂ storage was identified as the most robust solution across a wide range of operating conditions, while alternative transport modes provide flexibility for shorter distances.

Mega Heat Pump Economic Optimization

ETH Zürich × Everllence (ex MAN Energy Solutions) - Zurich, Switzerland

Fall 2024 - Spring 2025

Developed a techno-economic model to evaluate the profitability of large-scale heat pumps participating in electricity balancing markets (FCR), in collaboration with Everllence (formerly MAN Energy Solutions). The work integrates thermodynamic system modeling, electricity market data, and operational constraints to determine optimal system sizing and dispatch strategies. A key focus was quantifying the value of flexibility, showing how excess capacity and thermal storage can be leveraged to provide grid services. Results demonstrate that participation in balancing markets can significantly enhance project profitability, depending on system design and market conditions.

Design of a High Temperature Heat Pump

University of Cyprus - Nicosia, Cyprus

Fall 2023 – Spring 2024

Designed a high-temperature heat pump system capable of delivering up to 90°C for industrial and heating applications. Developed a detailed thermodynamic model to analyze the impact of key parameters such as refrigerant selection, mass flow rate, and system pressure on performance. The study included system optimization, efficiency analysis, and validation against expected thermodynamic behavior. Results highlight the critical role of refrigerant selection and operating conditions in achieving high system efficiency and reliable performance.
